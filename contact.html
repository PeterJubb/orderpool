Safety & trust

Order Pool is designed to be transparent without exposing sensitive details. Public tracking is status-only, while order execution stays anchored to private dashboards and updates.

Public vs private

Privacy policy →

What’s public

Accepted orders can be visible publicly with status-only tracking (e.g., accepted, in progress, fulfilled). Public views are designed to build trust without revealing private communications.

What’s private

Brief details, sensitive requirements, pricing, and manufacturer-to-publisher updates live inside the platform and appear on dashboards based on role permissions.

How updates work

Manufacturers update the order from the order detail page. Those updates syndicate to the publisher dashboard to reduce back-and-forth and keep execution aligned.

Trust mechanisms

The platform’s structure is meant to prevent ambiguity, reduce disputes, and keep work moving.

✓
Explicit states
Orders move through clear states from acceptance to fulfillment.
✓
Single source of truth
The order is anchored to the original brief plus an update stream.
✓
Role-based visibility
Publishers and manufacturers see what they need, public sees status only.
✓
Auditability
Updates are timestamped and tied to the order’s lifecycle.

Guidelines

Terms →
Publishers

Use clear specs and constraints. Treat the brief as the anchor for execution. Track progress through the dashboard rather than email threads.

Manufacturers

Accept work you can deliver. Post milestone updates in the order detail page so publishers stay aligned. Keep sensitive details inside the platform.

Public

Public tracking exists to show progress and accountability. It is not a place for negotiations, pricing, or private communications.
